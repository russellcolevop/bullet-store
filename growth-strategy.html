Bullet Trade Services — Growth Strategy

Growth Strategy

How we take this from zero to $500K+ in Year 1 — every channel, every dollar, every expected return.

The Core Thesis

Bullet Trade Services already has everything a typical e-commerce startup would spend 12 months trying to build: a credible brand, trade certification, an established customer base, a WooCommerce store, and a warm college pilot lead. What's missing is distribution — the systems that bring buyers to the store and turn visits into orders.

This strategy stacks five independent acquisition channels, each with its own economics and payback period. None of them require Phil's time. All of them compound.

The Five Channels

1. SEO & Content

Owned • Compounds • Slow

12 pillar articles per quarter targeting trade-specific long-tail keywords. Apprentice tool buying guides, trade school kit comparisons, brand face-offs (Knipex vs Klein, Fluke vs Ideal).

CAC: ~$12 Payback: 6–9 mo Cost: $0 (Russell)

2. Google Ads

Paid • Immediate • Scalable

Branded search (defensive), category terms ("millwright tool kit"), and shopping campaigns on pro-tier kits. Lowest CPC in the trades vertical.

CAC: ~$140 Payback: instant Budget: $2–3K/mo

3. Institutional / College

B2B • High-ticket • Defensible

Trade colleges buy in bulk (20–150 kits at a time) for cohort programs. Existing pilot is the lighthouse customer — 2 more colleges = $150K+ of predictable revenue.

CAC: ~$20 (outreach only) ACV: $75K+ Cost: Russell time

4. Email & Lifecycle

Owned • Recurring • Retention

Abandoned cart, post-purchase education, seasonal campaigns, apprentice-year upgrade nudges. Typical e-commerce attribution: 15–25% of revenue.

Target: 20% of rev Cost: $30/mo tool

5. Trade & Referral

Organic • Network • Trust

Leverage Bullet's existing industrial client network — shop leads, foremen, HR at partner companies. Referral program pays kickbacks on institutional deals.

CAC: ~$0 Conv: 2–3x org

6. Trade Media / PR

Earned • Credibility • Long-tail

Guest articles, industry publications, trade school newsletters. Goal isn't direct sales — it's defensive credibility that supports every other channel.

CAC: ~$0 Timing: Quarter 2

12-Month Rollout

1

Foundation (Month 0–1)

Pre-launch

Lock kit pricing, import products into WooCommerce, connect analytics and pixels, set up email platform, launch institutional outreach list. No ad spend yet.

2

Launch & First Revenue (Month 1–3)

Quarter 1

Google Ads live at $1.5K/mo, first 6 SEO articles published, email automations active, warm outreach to 20 colleges. Target: $60–80K revenue in Q1.

3

Scale Paid + Institutional (Month 4–6)

Quarter 2

Ad budget to $3K/mo on working campaigns, 2nd college pilot signed, first bulk order shipped, trade media pitches in flight. Target: $120–160K revenue in Q2.

4

Optimize (Month 7–9)

Quarter 3

SEO traffic compounds, email revenue hits 20% of total, repeat-customer flows activated, affiliate/referral program launched. Target: $160–200K revenue in Q3.

5

Expand (Month 10–12)

Quarter 4

Third and fourth institutional accounts close, seasonal Q4 push (back-to-apprenticeship), Year 2 planning. Target: $200K+ revenue in Q4.
