Bullet Trade Services — Financial Model

24-Month Financial Model

Three scenarios built off the confirmed $4,400 / $2,150 anchor kit and 51% blended gross margin.

Core Assumptions

  • Anchor kit: Millwright Pro — $4,400 retail / $2,150 landed cost / $2,250 GM (51%) — Phil-confirmed.
  • Blended GM: 51% across all six tiers (starter, pro, master × MW/EL).
  • Average order value: ~$3,500 (weighted toward pro tier).
  • Mix assumption: 55% pro, 25% starter, 20% master.
  • Fulfillment cost: handled internally by Bullet — modeled as included in landed cost.
  • Customer acquisition cost (CAC): $85–140 blended (SEO free, paid $180, referral/institutional $20).
  • Return rate: 3% (industry standard for trade tool sales).
  • Payment processing: 2.9% of revenue.
  • Platform costs: $150/month (WooCommerce hosting, plugins, email).

Break-Even Math

The e-commerce channel breaks even the moment the first kit ships. Every kit is profitable on a unit-economics basis — there is no negative margin period. What Phil is risking is inventory float (buying stock before it sells), not operating losses.

What Could Break This

Numbers are only as good as the assumptions. Here's what I'd stress-test with Phil:

  • Landed cost drift. If actual COGS comes in 10% higher, margin compresses to ~45% — still healthy but tighter.
  • CAC inflation. If paid channels cost 2x our estimate, break-even shifts by ~5 kits/month — manageable.
  • College pilot doesn't convert. Removes ~$175K from base case Year 1 revenue; still profitable, slower growth.
  • Competitor enters. Low probability — the trade-certified angle is a defensible moat.
