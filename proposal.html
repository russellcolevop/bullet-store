A Zero-Cost Growth Partnership for Bullet's E-Commerce Division

Build a new $500K–$1M revenue stream without writing a cheque until the business is already producing.

The One-Paragraph Version

Bullet Trade Services has a real e-commerce opportunity: a proven domain, certified credibility, existing WooCommerce infrastructure, an active college pilot, and zero direct competition in the comprehensive trade tool kit category. What it's missing is someone to run growth — the SEO, paid ads, email, content, institutional outreach, and analytics work that turns a store into a revenue engine. I'm offering to do that work in exchange for a phantom equity stake and profit share, with no cash required from Bullet upfront. You risk nothing until the business is already producing margin that wouldn't exist without me.

Rather than charging Bullet a fee or salary, I'm foregoing all upfront compensation in exchange for a defined stake in the e-commerce division's economics. Here's the math, shown transparently:

The risk multiplier reflects a standard convention in sweat-equity deals: because I'm taking on 100% of the execution risk with zero guaranteed cash, the foregone dollars convert at a premium. A 2.5x multiplier is the midpoint of the market-standard 1.5x–3x range.

What the $780K of equity credit translates to

Applied against the expected 2-year value of the e-commerce division (conservatively estimated at $2.5M–$3.5M based on the financial model), $780K represents approximately 25% of division value. The proposal:

  • 25% phantom equity stake in the e-commerce division's economic value — paid as distributions or via buyout, but not issued as actual shares (no restructuring of Bullet Trade Services required)
  • 10% of gross margin as ongoing profit share, paid quarterly, for as long as I'm actively running growth operations
  • Two-year vesting schedule on the phantom equity: 50% vests at 12 months, 100% at 24 months — protects both sides
  • Anti-dilution protection on the 25% stake; it cannot be reduced without my written consent
  • Buyout clause (detailed below) giving Phil a clean exit path at any time
Why phantom equity instead of real shares: Real shares in Bullet Trade Services would mean diluting your ownership of the entire 100-person industrial business — which is not what either of us wants. Phantom equity is a contract that gives me all the economic rights of equity (distributions, buyout, anti-dilution) without any legal restructuring of your existing company. It's a standard structure for sweat-equity deals against an established business adding a new division.

Vesting, Buyout, and Exit Scenarios

Vesting schedule

The 25% phantom equity does not vest instantly. It vests on a 24-month schedule to protect Phil from a scenario where I disengage after a few months.

  • Months 1–11: 0% vested (cliff period)
  • Month 12: 12.5% vests
  • Months 13–23: Remaining 12.5% vests linearly (approximately 1.04% per month)
  • Month 24: Fully vested at 25%

Buyout clause

At any point after Month 12, Phil may buy out my phantom equity stake and terminate the profit share. The buyout price is calculated as the greater of:

  • Formula A: 4x trailing twelve-month profit share payments (covers the ongoing cash flow I'm giving up), plus
  • Formula B: 25% of (3x trailing twelve-month gross margin) — the implied division value at that point in time

This gives Phil a clean, pre-agreed exit at any time without ambiguity. It also gives me a defined downside case: even if the partnership ends prematurely, I have a formulaic claim to compensation for the work I've already performed.

What happens if…

…the business doesn't work?

If Year 1 revenue falls below $150K, either party may terminate the partnership with 30 days notice. Any vested phantom equity remains, but paid out at then-current (reduced) valuation. Phil's downside is limited to what he actually spent on inventory.

…Russell disengages?

If I stop performing (defined as <15 hrs/week average over any 60-day period) without cause, Phil may trigger a clawback of unvested equity and reduce profit share to zero. Vested equity remains but is paid at buyout formula.

…Phil wants to sell the whole business?

If Bullet Trade Services is sold entirely, my phantom equity converts to cash at the buyout formula price, calculated against the e-commerce division's proportional contribution to sale price.

…we want to restructure later?

If the e-commerce business scales past $2M in revenue, we can mutually agree to spin it into a separate NewCo with real share issuance. The phantom equity converts to actual shares at a 1:1 ratio.

Phil's cash exposure is bounded by inventory. My exposure is unbounded in labor. That asymmetry is what the 2.5x multiplier is pricing in.
